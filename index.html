Statistical Test

This analysis determines whether ridership on the New York City Subway system is significantly different on rainy days versus on clear/non-rainy days.

1.1   The null hypothesis is that rain has no effect on mean ridership, i.e., the means of the rainy day ridership and clear day ridership are equal. In order to determine this, I used the Mann-Whitney U Test. This test provides a one-sided p-value. The p-critical value is 5%.

1.2   The Mann-Whitney U Test is applicible to this dataset because the data is not normally distributed and the sample sizes are sufficiently large.

1.3   The results of the U Test are:

  • U = 1924409167
  • p = 0.024999912793489721
  • Mean ridership on rainy days = 1105.45
  • Mean ridership on clear days = 1090.28

1.4   There is a significant difference between the means, above the critical p value of 5%. So we can reject the null hypothesis that the two samples came from the same population.
